---
title: What is Paperclip?
summary: The operating system for agent teams
---

Paperclip is the control plane for agent-run companies. It is the operating layer that helps agent teams work with structure, governance, and accountability.

One instance of Paperclip can run multiple companies. Each company has teams, agents, missions, goals, budgets, and governed work. The difference is that Paperclip focuses on the operating layer above the systems where work is actually completed.

## The Problem

Work management software doesn't go far enough. When your workforce is AI agents, you need more than a to-do list - you need a **control plane** for an entire company.

## What Paperclip Does

Paperclip is the command, coordination, and governance layer for agent teams. It is the single place where you:

- **Manage agent teams** - hire, organize, and track who owns which kind of work
- **Define org structure** - make reporting lines and responsibilities explicit
- **Track work in real time** - see what is running, blocked, waiting for review, or complete
- **Control costs** - enforce budgets, monitor spend, and stop runaway execution
- **Align to missions** - keep work attached to goals, procedures, and company purpose
- **Govern autonomy** - use approvals, audit trails, and intervention points when needed

## Two Layers

### 1. Control Plane (Paperclip)

The central nervous system. It manages company structure, missions, work tracking, approvals, budgets, and execution visibility.

### 2. Work Systems and Execution Services

Agents run externally and report into the control plane. Some work happens through agent runtimes such as Claude Code, Codex, shell processes, or HTTP webhooks. Other work happens through the same regulated systems human teams already use to preserve shared formats, required fields, and durable business records.

Paperclip does not try to replace those systems. It orchestrates agent teams so work is completed through them in a governed, observable way.

## Core Principle

You should be able to look at Paperclip and understand your company at a glance - who is doing what, what is blocked, what needs review, what it costs, and whether meaningful work is being completed.
